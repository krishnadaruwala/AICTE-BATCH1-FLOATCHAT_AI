# Barrier layer

A barrier layer is a layer of water between the base of the shallow,
salinity-controlled mixed layer and the top of the thermocline. It forms where
fresh surface water (from heavy rainfall or river runoff) creates a shallow
density-controlled mixed layer that is shallower than the isothermal layer.

Because the barrier layer is warm but isolated from the cooler thermocline
below, it suppresses vertical mixing and entrainment of cool water into the
mixed layer. This can trap heat near the surface and is important for air-sea
interaction and tropical cyclone intensification.

The Bay of Bengal is a classic example: monsoon freshwater input produces a
strong barrier layer for much of the year, whereas the more saline Arabian Sea
generally has a weaker or absent barrier layer.
